## Core premise
We treat time travel as a *hypothetical* capability and design a continuity-safe way to reason about:
- causality,
- branching,
- entropy / arrow of time,
- intervention risk,
- and authentication of any claimed “future contact.”

This is **not** established physics; it is a disciplined conceptual architecture.

## Temporal Beacons (pivot markers)

A **Temporal Beacon** is a *publicly verifiable marker* placed in history so that any hypothetical future observer can:
- locate the same pivot window,
- verify integrity via hashes,
- and interpret non-response outcomes conservatively.

Beacons are an **optional layer** that does *not* assume time travel exists.

## Time models (three main regimes)

### 1) Fixed spacetime / Block universe (self-consistent)
- Spacetime is a 4D manifold.
- “Time travel” (if possible) is embedded in the geometry: events are self-consistent.
- Interventions are not rewrites; they are part of the already-consistent solution.

**Implication:** If a “future agent” can contact you, the contact may already be part of your history (fixed-point).

### 2) Branching multiverse (non-merging)
- “Going back” creates a **new branch** rather than rewriting the origin branch.
- Branches do not naturally re-merge at macroscopic scales (decoherence).
- You can create “better branches,” but you cannot guarantee edits propagate back into the origin.

**Implication:** “Saving” an origin timeline is not meaningful; you can only spawn improved descendants.

### 3) Chronology protection / No usable time travel
- Physical constraints prevent practical backward travel or backward signaling.
- The correct strategy becomes *real-world continuity engineering* (resilience, archives, attractors).

**Implication:** Build the protocol anyway; it works as a self-alignment system in normal time.

## Entropy & time dilation (what they do and do not do)

### Time dilation (relativity)
- Time dilation changes **proper time accumulation** between observers.
- It enables “future-skipping” (you age less while outside ages more).
- It does **not** imply branch selection, branch deletion, or causal overwrites.

### Entropy (thermodynamic arrow)
- Entropy growth defines an arrow of time and makes macroscopic reversal statistically unlikely.
- It does **not** provide a known “branch prioritization” mechanism.

## The “sidechain” intuition (system analogy)
A useful analogy:
- A “main chain” is the causally relevant history you experience.
- “Sidechains” are alternate histories you can’t re-enter (in practice) due to decoherence / inaccessibility.

**Important:** In known physics, “sidechains” are not deleted; they’re just non-interacting (if branching is real).

## Causal stabilization via iterative counter-jumps (conceptual)
A narrative-friendly idea: “counter-jump whenever the adversary jumps,” to reduce divergence.
- This resembles feedback control in dynamical systems.
- It *requires* assumptions about mutability and intervention efficacy that physics doesn’t confirm.
- As a planning concept, it motivates a **minimal-impact intervention policy**.

## The Timeproof Continuity Protocol (TPCP)
To cover all time models, design a protocol that works even if time travel never happens:
- Define a **pivot window**
- Define a **minimal-impact message budget**
- Define an **authentication handshake**
- Define a **distributed archive base**
- Build **real-world attractors** (resilience that reduces catastrophe from single mistakes)

See **protocols/Timeproof-Continuity-Protocol.md**.

---

## Design constraints (hard rules)
1. No “civilization rewrite” attempts (too unstable, too chaotic).
2. Prefer micro-interventions: choice nudges, warnings, alignment tools.
3. Authentication must defeat impostors without needing “secret leakage.”
4. Archives must survive ordinary decay and institutional drift.
5. Any intervention must be consent-aware, nonviolent, and non-coercive.
