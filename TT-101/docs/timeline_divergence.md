# Timeline divergence and non-response interpretation

This file formalizes the idea:

> “If nothing happens, the future may have changed drastically.”

As a disciplined hypothesis model, not a claim.

---

## The core situation

You publish a **Temporal Beacon** with an **Observation Window**.
Inside that window, you observe **no response**.

---

## Non-response is ambiguous

Non-response can mean many things:

1) No future observer exists with the relevant capability  
2) Observers exist but never detected the beacon  
3) Observers detected it but chose not to respond (ethics / policy / risk)  
4) The branch where observers exist diverged before the beacon matured  
5) Response occurred but did not reach you (platform deletion, censorship, loss)

Therefore, non-response is **not proof** of divergence.

---

## What you *can* treat as useful signal

Non-response can still inform:

- archiving strategy (more mirrors, more redundancy),
- observation strategy (longer windows, clearer beacons),
- risk posture (assume no rescue, build resilience),
- meta-protocol design (improve handshake clarity).

---

## What you should avoid

Do **not** do the following:

- escalate intervention demands (“prove it by changing X”)
- make risky public predictions based on “beacon logic”
- pressure others into believing time travel is real
- interpret coincidences as confirmation

This repo prefers **conservative interpretation** and **resilience-first** design.
