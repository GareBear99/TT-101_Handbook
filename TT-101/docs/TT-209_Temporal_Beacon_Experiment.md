# TT-209 — Temporal Beacon Experiment (Temporal-SETI framing)

> “Knowledge preserved is civilization extended.”
> — Unknown Archivist

---


This document frames TT-101 publishing as an experiment in **signaling discipline**.

It does not assume time travel exists.

---

## Hypothesis (non-claim)

If future observers (human or otherwise) can scan history for intentional signals,
then a well-structured, integrity-verifiable archive may be detectable.

---

## Prediction

A beacon with:

- clear pivot window,
- explicit listener contract,
- and cryptographic integrity artifacts

is more likely to be:
- correctly interpreted,
- safely responded to (if ever),
- or safely ignored without harm.

---

## Experiment method

1) Publish TT-101 publicly (repo + release)
2) Register a first beacon entry (TB-000 or TB-001)
3) Mirror the release to at least one additional archive
4) Record SHA-256 hashes in `manifest/hashes.txt`
5) Observe passively through the Observation Window

---

## Measurements (low-risk)

- repository survival over years
- mirror consistency
- community forks / citations
- integrity verification outcomes

If a response occurs, treat it as:
- untrusted until authenticated,
- and bounded by the minimal message budget.

---

## Safety posture

This experiment must remain:
- non-coercive,
- non-violent,
- non-financially exploitative,
- and robust against prank / hoax responses.
