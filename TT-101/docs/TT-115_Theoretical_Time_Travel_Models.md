# TT-115 — Theoretical Time Travel Models (Physics-Limited)

> “The past, like the future, is indefinite and exists only as a spectrum of possibilities.”
> — Stephen Hawking

---

Time travel is common in fiction, but physics treats it as an **extreme, constrained question**.
This segment summarizes the most commonly discussed **theoretical mechanisms** and the **rules/constraints** that would have to hold for them to exist.

The purpose here is not to claim time travel is achievable, but to explain:

- what physics *allows in principle* (in certain models)
- what it *seems to prohibit in practice*
- why **TT-101** focuses on continuity of **knowledge**, artifacts, and verification rather than assuming transport of people

---

## 1) Forward “Time Travel” via Relativity (Verified)

**Relativistic time dilation** is experimentally confirmed.
If you move fast enough (relative to someone else) or sit deeper in a gravitational field, your clock runs **slower** relative to theirs.

Result:
- you can arrive in the *future* of others while experiencing less personal time
- this does **not** provide a known mechanism for traveling into your own past

This is the only “time travel” effect with strong experimental support.

---

## 2) Closed Timelike Curves (CTCs) (Theoretical)

In general relativity, spacetime can, in some mathematical solutions, contain paths that loop back in time—**closed timelike curves**.

Examples discussed in the literature include:
- Gödel-type rotating universe solutions
- certain spacetime geometries around extreme rotating masses

Constraints / issues:
- these solutions often require conditions that may not exist in our universe
- many physicists suspect nature prevents stable CTC formation

---

## 3) Wormholes as Time Bridges (Theoretical)

A wormhole is a hypothetical shortcut through spacetime.
If one “mouth” of a wormhole experiences a different time rate than the other (due to velocity or gravity), the two ends could become time-shifted.

In principle:
- traveling through the wormhole could connect different times

Constraints / issues:
- wormholes are not observed
- stability likely requires “exotic” energy conditions (negative energy densities)
- quantum effects may collapse the structure

---

## 4) Cosmic Strings / Tipler Cylinder (Highly Speculative)

Other proposals involve exotic objects:
- **cosmic strings** (hypothetical ultra-dense defects)
- a **Tipler cylinder** (a massive infinitely long rotating cylinder)

Constraints:
- unobserved objects
- unrealistic mass/geometry requirements
- often imply unphysical boundary conditions

---

## 5) Chronology Protection (Why Nature May Block the Past)

A major idea is that the laws of physics may prevent backwards time travel from becoming usable.
One common framing is **chronology protection**: the universe disallows paradox-enabling time loops via instability or runaway quantum effects.

Practical meaning for this handbook:
- even if equations allow CTCs in certain solutions, reality may never permit them to form stably

---

## Rule Set (If Backwards Time Travel Were Possible)

If a backward-time mechanism existed, it would likely require all of the following:

1. **Stable spacetime structure** (wormhole/CTC geometry that doesn’t collapse)
2. **Coordinate targeting** (arrive at the right spacetime coordinates, not just “the right year”)
3. **Paradox handling** (self-consistency or branching models)
4. **Energy feasibility** (requirements within achievable bounds)
5. **Detectability** (observable signatures or constraints)
