This section maps TT-101/301/401/501 into a broader progression model.

One Practical Stage Model (Condensed)

S0) Pre-industrial local societies

S1) Industrial civilization (mass production, global logistics)

S2) Digital civilization (planetary computation + networks)

S3) Automated fabrication civilization (robots + fab networks)

S4) Multi-planet civilization (redundancy across worlds)

S5) Interstellar civilization (delay-tolerant, ultra-distributed)

S6) Deep-time civilization (10,000+ year continuity, multiple collapse cycles)

Where we are

We are between **S2** and early **S3**:

  • high computation and networks
  • limited robotics penetration
  • early consumer fabrication (3D printers)
  • weak long-horizon governance

How TT-101 Contributes

  • TT-101: decision & continuity protocols (information layer)
  • TT-301: physical survivability (passive-first)
  • TT-401: site/legal/ethical constraints (institutional survivability)
  • TT-501: collapse resilience (reboot pathway)
  • TT-60x: expansion to multi-node civilization infrastructure

Key Claim (Regardless of Time Travel)

If time travel never exists, TT-101 is still a blueprint for:

  • robust knowledge persistence
  • civilizational reboot readiness
  • staged automation/fabrication toward redundancy
