What counts as “binary” here?
Each letter is a byte: 01000001 = ASCII A. Clues show one byte per letter, separated by spaces.

Why this belongs in TT-300

If your slate workflow ever requires manual reconstruction, the failure mode is rarely “no data” — it’s “some bytes are known but you must interpret them correctly.” This puzzle trains byte-to-letter decoding and pattern recognition without relying on external tooling.
