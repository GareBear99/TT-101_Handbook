TT-324 — Self-Fabrication & Self-Sufficiency Doctrine (v1)

> “Knowledge preserved is civilization extended.”

> — Unknown Archivist

---

This tier defines what “self-sufficient” means **without fantasy**.

Core Rule

The system must survive using only:

  • matter
  • energy
  • information
  • physical laws

It must not depend on currency, institutions, or supply chains as permanent prerequisites.

Money is treated as a **bootstrap interface** for the current era only.

The 5 Closed Loops

A continuity system is “self-sufficient” only when it can close these loops over time:

1) **Energy loop**

  • minimum viable: solar + mechanical fallback + direct-use operation
  • archive core must survive with **zero power** (optical slate)

2) **Material loop**

  • prioritize stone, ceramics, and recyclable metals
  • avoid rare single-point materials where possible

3) **Tooling loop**

Self-fabrication is **tiered**, not absolute:

  • L0: hand tools + manual engraving
  • L1: mechanical jig/CNC engraving
  • L2: laser engraving (if available)
  • L3: additive manufacturing of non-electronic parts
  • L4: electronics assembly using preserved components
  • L5: industrial reproduction (requires civilization-scale capability)

Hard truth:

  • motors, magnets, chips are not realistically “ore-to-chip” in a bunker.
  • therefore L4/L5 require either stored spares or a functioning industrial base.

This is acceptable because L0–L3 preserve continuity.

4) **Information loop**

  • TT-999 + TT-308 must exist in passive form
  • all fabrication processes must have:
  • minimal-tool pathway
  • verification (hash/check) pathway

5) **Governance loop**

  • no “chosen authority”
  • distributed verification beats leadership
  • fail-closed incident response is mandatory

Completion Criteria

TT-324 is satisfied when the archive can:

  • persist without power (L0)
  • be reproduced without high technology (L0/L1)
  • be validated without network access (manual decode + hash)
