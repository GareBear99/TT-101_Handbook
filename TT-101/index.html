Goal
Maximize the chance of a low-collateral “future-to-past” intervention that helps you at a pivotal moment without destabilizing broader history.
Assumption-agnostic

Recommended workflow:
  1. Finalize your Pivot Window
  2. Finalize Handshake (phrase + artifact + behavioral)
  3. Write the one-page Intervention Card
  4. Append-only canon log updates
Intervention Card (one-page rule) Keep it minimal-impact. 
RULES FOR ANY “FUTURE CONTACT” CLAIM:

1) AUTH FIRST
   - No handshake proof => no trust, no action.
2) MINIMAL MESSAGE BUDGET
   - 1 identity proof
   - 1 direction
   - 1 warning
   - 1 tool / checklist
   - No tech dumps, no public demos, no money/market tips.
3) LOCALITY
   - Deliver privately; avoid public ripples.
4) LOG IT
   - Record time/place/claims; add to append-only canon log.

If time travel is impossible, this becomes a self-alignment artifact for the Pivot Window.
TT-101 is a conceptual systems framework inspired by time-travel fiction and real-world robustness engineering. It is not an endorsement of the physical feasibility of time travel.
